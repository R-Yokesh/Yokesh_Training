Rules & Regulations

  • Each team consists of 11 players, including one goalkeeper. Teams must also have substitutes available for replacements.


  • A standard football match lasts 90 minutes, divided into two 45-minute halves with a 15-minute halftime break. Additional time may be added for stoppages.


  • Teams are allowed to make a certain number of substitutions (usually three in most competitions). Some tournaments might allow more, especially in knockout stages or due to specific circumstances like extra time.


  • A player is in an offside position if they are nearer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-last opponent when the ball is played to them, and they are actively involved in the play.


  • Referees enforce rules against fouls, such as tripping, pushing, and handling the ball. Yellow cards are given as cautions, while red cards result in immediate expulsion from the game.


  • A goal is scored when the entire ball crosses the goal line between the goalposts and beneath the crossbar, provided no infringements have occurred.


  • Direct and indirect free kicks are awarded for various infringements. Penalty kicks are awarded for fouls committed within the penalty area.
